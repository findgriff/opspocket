The terminal is the foundation. But when you're running multiple AI services, Clawbot instances, Docker stacks, and VPS servers across different machines — you need more than a command line. You need a dashboard that sees everything, all at once. That's Mission Control.

What Is Mission Control?

Mission Control is the next major feature coming to OpsPocket — a visual, always-on monitoring and management layer that sits on top of your SSH connections and OpenClaw infrastructure. Instead of opening a terminal and running openclaw status on each server manually, Mission Control gives you a single screen where everything is visible at a glance.

Imagine your OpenClaw agent is down, your gateway hasn't restarted since last Tuesday, and a new skill update is available — Mission Control surfaces all of that the moment you open the app, without you having to go looking.

What You'll Be Able to Do

Here's what's being built inside Mission Control right now:

🟢

Live Service Health

At-a-glance status indicators for every OpenClaw gateway, agent, and service across all your servers. Green is good. Red means action needed.

🤖

Agent Overview

See all your Clawbot and OpenClaw agents in one grid — name, status, last activity, skill count, and a quick restart button without entering a terminal.

📊

Resource Snapshots

CPU, memory, and disk at a glance for every connected server. Know before something breaks, not after.

🔔

Alerting

Push notifications when a gateway goes down, a service restarts unexpectedly, or a disk threshold is crossed — without needing a separate monitoring tool.

⚡

One-Tap Remediation

See a problem, fix it. Tap a failing service to restart it, view its logs, or SSH directly into it — all from the Mission Control screen.

📅

Cron & Scheduled Tasks

A visual view of all OpenClaw cron jobs across your infrastructure — when they last ran, when they run next, and whether they succeeded.

🖥️

SSH Terminal

Already live. Full interactive terminal with JetBrains Mono, ANSI colour, and Face ID protection — available right now in OpsPocket.

⚡

Slash Commands

Already live. 60+ built-in commands for Docker, PM2, systemd, tmux and OpenClaw — type / to launch the palette.

Why It Matters

The people building and running AI services on OpenClaw are operating at the frontier of what's possible with autonomous agents and infrastructure automation. The tools they use to manage that infrastructure need to match the sophistication of what they're running.

Right now, checking on your Clawbot means SSH-ing in, running a few commands, checking a log file, and repeating across every server. That's fine for a developer session. It's exhausting when you're doing it at 11pm on your phone because something sent you an alert.

Mission Control collapses all of that into a single screen. One look, full picture.
